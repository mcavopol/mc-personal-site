## Narrative Bio (~500 words)

> I love the moment when a chaotic situation starts to make sense — when you can see through the noise to the few things that actually matter. That's been the thread through 20 years in B2B SaaS.
>
> I started as an engineer at LeanKit, one of the earliest Agile/Lean project management tools. I quickly realized my passion wasn't writing code — it was using technology to solve problems. I moved from engineering into product, then built professional services when I saw that customers had the data but not the insight. That PS team became customer success, which became the majority of company revenue. We grew from $3M to $18M ARR before Planview acquired us. Customers included Walmart, BAE Systems, and Rolls-Royce.
>
> At Ambition, I learned the hardest lesson of my career. The founders had built for the buyer, not the user. The product should have been a Salesforce integration, not a standalone dashboard. You can paper over product gaps with great customer success — but only for so long. I tripled ARR from $2M to $6M, pushed NRR up 30 points, and drove expansion ARR 12.7x. But the experience hardened a conviction about JTBD and user-first product thinking that's driven every role since.
>
> Fresh Technology was the proving ground. Restaurant tech through a pandemic. I walked in, saw that delivery marketplaces were doing to restaurants what Amazon did to booksellers, and built a strategic narrative around it. Then we built the products — first-party online ordering and a kitchen display system for demand routing. I identified that POS partnerships were the growth unlock and signed Square, Clover, and Lightspeed. Sales cycles dropped from 8 months to 15 days. Revenue grew 93% year-over-year.
>
> Now I'm COO & CPO at Arena Labs, where the work is AI-native from the ground up. We're building healthcare workforce wellness products that pair human coaches with AI. Think of it like Tesla's Autopilot model — coaches generate training data while coaching, AI runs recommendations in the background, and we pre-compile conversation starters to reduce cognitive load. The result: 85% engagement versus a 3-5% industry baseline. We're not replacing humans. We're using AI to scale quality.
>
> Through all of it, one thing has stayed constant: I lead from the front. I get on calls. I pick up the phone. I get on the plane. I hire for human characteristics over credentials — a math teacher who became a support agent who became a product manager; an events coordinator who rose to lead the entire CS function. People I've hired consistently rise to leadership positions years later.
>
> On the side, I run Hedgehog Growth Partners, advising non-tech "Main Street" companies on practical AI strategy. My thesis: AI is the biggest unlock for companies that aren't in tech. Every business owner needs to answer three questions fast — how does AI grow revenue, how does it improve margins, and how does it transform what you actually offer customers?
>
> B.S. Computer Science, MTSU. Nashville-based. Married to Laura Lea, father to Luca.
