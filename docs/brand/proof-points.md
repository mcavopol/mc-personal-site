## Roles

### Arena Labs — CPO & COO (2024–present)

- **Pillars:** P1, P2
- **Situation:** Pro services company that had failed multiple times to become a product company. Selling 8-week coaching programs to clinicians with discretionary budgets instead of C-suite P&L owners. Linear content product with bolt-on features.
- **Thesis:** Pivot GTM to hospital C-suite buyers (CNOs, CMOs, CEOs, CFOs). Rebuild product around JTBD: (1) acute intervention — physiological tools for in-the-moment stress, (2) longitudinal habit formation — personalized protocol toolkit. $25–45/mo subscription profitable without human coach.
- **Key outcomes:**
  - 85% engagement vs. 3–5% industry baseline
  - AI strategy: coaches generate training data → AI runs recommendations in background → pre-compiles conversation starters to reduce coach cognitive load (Tesla Autopilot model)

### Prado — Interim COO & SVP Revenue/Product (2023–2024)

- **Pillars:** P1, P3
- **Situation:** High-burn startup serving two conflicting ICPs (enterprise headless OMS vs. SMB all-in-one). Engineering spread thin. Embedded website builder forcing hosting on Prado infrastructure, blocking enterprise customers.
- **Thesis:** 20% of logos = 90% of revenue → pivot to enterprise ICP. Free resources to build payments system (Rainforest Payments), remove Stripe/Square as middlemen.
- **Key outcomes:**
  - Burn: 3x → 0x in 6 months
  - Median contract value: +427x
  - Dev cycle: 60 days → <7 days
  - OpEx reduction: 70%

### Fresh Technology — SVP Revenue & Product (2020–2024)

- **Pillars:** P1, P2
- **Situation:** Legacy mom-and-pop IT services company. COVID hit right as VC raise was happening. Delivery marketplaces (Uber Eats, DoorDash, GrubHub) disintermediating restaurants the way Amazon disintermediated booksellers.
- **Thesis:** Channel distribution via POS partnerships is the unlock. Built strategic narrative for customers to understand the shift. Pulled KDS off the shelf — became critical for demand routing, margin-aware prioritization, delivery driver coordination, ETA accuracy.
- **Key outcomes:**
  - Sales cycle: 8 months → 15 days
  - 93% YoY growth
  - Support SLA: 23x faster
  - Signed Square, Clover, Lightspeed as channel partners

### Ambition — VP Post-Sales & Solutions (2017–2020)

- **Pillars:** P1, P2, P3
- **Situation:** Employee ~10. Founders built for the buyer, not the user. Product should have been a Salesforce integration, not a standalone dashboard.
- **Thesis:** Build post-sales machine to paper over product gaps while pushing for product-market fit. Professional services team as bridge.
- **Key outcomes:**
  - ARR: $2M → $6M (eventually $8M)
  - NRR: +30 points
  - Expansion ARR: 12.7x
  - NPS: 55
  - Built CS, SE, PS, and support functions from zero

### LeanKit — Director, CS & Professional Services (2015–2017)

- **Pillars:** P1, P2, P3
- **Situation:** Built custom reporting API for enterprise customers who needed performance data for budgeting/planning. Deployed it. Customers did nothing with it.
- **Thesis:** Gap wasn't data access — it was insight delivery. Customers weren't in the business of building reports. Built PS team with BI analysts to bridge capability-to-insight gap.
- **Key outcomes:**
  - PS team → support/docs oversight → customer success leadership → owned majority of company revenue at exit
  - CX NPS: 50+
  - Planview acquisition

### LeanKit — Product Manager, Construction (2013–2015)

- **Pillars:** P1, P2
- **Situation:** Joined as engineer (#~20). Agile/Lean transformation wave. Construction was one of the last industries ripe for Agile adoption.
- **Thesis:** Tiger team to open construction vertical from first principles — new market with massive upside.
- **Key outcomes:**
  - New vertical opened successfully
  - Customers: Walmart, BAE Systems, Rolls-Royce
  - Company ARR: $3M → $18M
