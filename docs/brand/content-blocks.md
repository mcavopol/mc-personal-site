## For Non-Tech Business Owners (HGP Audience)

**Emphasis:** AI thesis (3 questions), practical over theoretical, Main Street focus, plain language.

### Opening Block

I've spent 20 years building technology companies. Now I spend a lot of my time helping non-tech businesses figure out AI — not in a theoretical, "here's a whitepaper" kind of way, but practically. What should you actually do? Where do you start? What's real and what's hype?

### AI Thesis Block (Plain Language)

Here's the short version: software is about to get radically cheaper for everyone. AI-powered tools are going to do what your $50k/year SaaS stack does for a fraction of the cost. That's great news — but only if you're ready. Every business owner I talk to needs to answer three questions:

1. **How does AI help you sell more?** Not "add a chatbot." How does it fundamentally change how you find and close customers?
2. **How does AI cut your costs?** Where are you paying people to do work that AI can do faster and cheaper?
3. **How does AI change what you actually sell?** This is the big one. Not bolting AI onto your existing product — rethinking your offering from scratch.

Whether you run a restaurant, a manufacturing company, a retail brand, or a services firm — these questions apply.

### Credibility Block

I'm not a consultant who reads about AI. I build AI products every day at Arena Labs — healthcare software where AI and human coaches work together. I've also built and scaled four other technology companies over 20 years. I know what works in practice, not just in theory.

### Lead Proof Points
- Currently building AI-native products (Arena Labs) — not just advising
- 85% engagement vs. 3–5% industry baseline (AI product results)
- Four disruption cycles — pattern recognition for navigating change
- "Three questions" framework — simple, actionable, no jargon
